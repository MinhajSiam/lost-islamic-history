About the Book

From the barren deserts of pre-Islamic Arabia to the flourishing centres of learning in Baghdad, Córdoba, and Cairo — Lost Islamic History traces the extraordinary journey of Muslim civilisation across fourteen centuries.

Author Firas Alkhateeb challenges the modern disconnect between Muslims and their history, revealing the profound contributions of Islamic scholars in mathematics, astronomy, medicine, and philosophy that laid the foundations of the modern world. This is the story of a civilisation that changed humanity — and must not be forgotten.

Across the Centuries

Key Events in History

✦
570 CE

Birth of the Prophet ﷺ

Muhammad ibn Abdullah born in Mecca to the Banu Hashim clan of the Quraysh tribe — an orphan who would change the world.

610 CE

The First Revelation

In the cave of Hira, the angel Jibreel commands Muhammad: "Read!" — the first verses of the Quran descend. The Islamic movement is born.

622 CE

The Hijra — Islamic Calendar Begins

Prophet Muhammad emigrates from Mecca to Yathrib (Medina), founding the first Muslim state and marking Year One of the Islamic calendar.

624 CE

Battle of Badr

Against all odds, 313 Muslims defeat a 1,000-strong Quraysh army — establishing the Muslim community as a genuine military and political force.

630 CE

Conquest of Mecca

10,000 Muslims march on Mecca. The city surrenders nearly bloodlessly. 360 idols are destroyed. A remarkable era of mercy — former enemies are pardoned.

636 CE

Battle of Yarmuk

Khalid ibn al-Walid decisively defeats the Byzantine imperial army in present-day Jordan–Syria. The Byzantine Empire loses Syria permanently.

637 CE

Jerusalem Opens Its Gates

Caliph Umar personally accepts the surrender of Jerusalem. Jews are permitted to return to the Holy City for the first time in over 500 years.

711 CE

Spain & India in the Same Year

Tariq ibn Ziyad crosses into Iberia (Gibraltar) while Muhammad bin Qasim leads forces into Sindh (India) — the Umayyad empire reaches its greatest extent simultaneously.

762 CE

Baghdad Founded

Caliph Al-Mansur establishes Baghdad between the Tigris and Euphrates. Within 20 years it becomes the largest city in the world — over one million residents.

~830 CE

House of Wisdom Flourishes

Caliph Al-Ma'mun's Bayt al-Hikmah becomes the world's premier centre of science — library, university, translation institute, and research lab, all under one roof.

859 CE

World's Oldest University

The University of al-Qarawiyyin is founded in Fez, Morocco by Fatima al-Fihri — a Muslim woman. It continues operating to this day.

1099 CE

Crusaders Capture Jerusalem

After a week-long siege, Crusader forces take Jerusalem. The city's entire civilian population is massacred. A shattered Muslim world struggles to respond.

The Golden Age

Scholars Who Shaped the World

✦

These are the giants of the 9th–13th century Islamic Golden Age — mathematicians, physicians, physicists and geographers whose discoveries still underpin modern science.

🔢

Al-Khwarizmi

780 – 850 CE · Persia

Mathematics

Invented algebra (al-jabr) and popularised the Indian numeral system (0–9) across the world. The word algorithm derives from the Latin version of his name.

Key work: The Compendious Book on Calculation by Completion and Balancing

🏥

Ibn Sina (Avicenna)

980 – 1037 CE · Persia

Medicine

Proposed that disease spreads through air and water — 800 years before germ theory. His Canon of Medicine was a standard medical textbook in Europe until the 17th century.

Key work: The Canon of Medicine (Al-Qanun fi al-Tibb)

🔭

Ibn al-Haytham

965 – 1040 CE · Iraq / Egypt

Physics & Optics

Father of modern optics and pioneer of the scientific method. Invented the Camera Obscura. Concluded light travels in straight lines and enters the eye — not the reverse.

Key work: Book of Optics (Kitab al-Manazir)

📐

Omar Khayyam

1048 – 1131 CE · Persia

Mathematics

Solved cubic equations geometrically and formulated the binomial theorem. Designed a solar calendar more accurate than the Gregorian calendar used today.

Also celebrated for his philosophical poetry: The Rubaiyat

🗺️

Muhammad al-Idrisi

1100 – 1165 CE · Sicily

Geography

Created the most accurate world map of the Middle Ages — the Tabula Rogeriana — under the patronage of the Norman King Roger II of Sicily. It remained the benchmark for centuries.

Earth's circumference calculated to within 37 km of the actual value.

✨

Abu Hamid al-Ghazali

1058 – 1111 CE · Persia

Philosophy & Theology

Called the most influential Muslim after the Prophet and Companions. Reconciled orthodox Islamic theology with philosophical reasoning, and championed Sufism as a spiritual path.

Key work: The Revival of the Religious Sciences (Ihya' Ulum al-Din)

9th – 13th Century

The Intellectual Golden Age

✦

While Europe was in its so-called Dark Ages, the Muslim world was at the forefront of human knowledge — producing advances that form the basis of the modern scientific world.

∑

Mathematics

  • ◆

    Al-Khwarizmi invented algebra — the word itself derives from his book al-Jabr (820 CE)

  • ◆

    Introduced the Indian numeral system (0–9) and the concept of zero to the Western world

  • ◆

    Developed trigonometric tables to determine exact prayer direction (qibla) from any city on Earth — the basis of GPS geometry today

🌌

Astronomy

  • ◆

    First to separate astronomy from astrology — replacing mythology with empirical observation

  • ◆

    Al-Biruni (11th c.) proposed the Earth rotates on its axis — 500 years before Copernicus and Galileo

  • ◆

    Perfected the astrolabe for precise navigation — the standard instrument until the 1700s

⚕️

Medicine

  • ◆

    Ibn Sina identified disease transmission through air, water, and soil — the first germ theory, ~800 years early

  • ◆

    Baghdad's free public hospitals (9th c.) had specialists, surgical wards, psychiatric care, and maternity units

  • ◆

    Ibn al-Nafis (13th c.) accurately described pulmonary blood circulation — 300 years before William Harvey

⚗️

Physics & Technology

  • ◆

    Ibn al-Haytham established the scientific method — empirical observation over philosophical speculation

  • ◆

    Camera Obscura invented — the direct ancestor of modern photography and cinema

  • ◆

    Al-Jazari (12th c.) engineered water-powered automata, crankshafts, and programmable musical robots
